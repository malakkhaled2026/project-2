# Procrastination Behavior Analysis

A data analysis project investigating the relationship between procrastination habits and academic/work performance among students.

## Project Overview

This project explores how procrastination patterns, digital distractions, and time management behaviors affect performance and work quality.
The study was conducted using survey data collected from 85 participants.

## Research Question

To what extent does procrastination negatively affect academic and professional performance, and what are the main causes of procrastination behavior?

## Hypothesis

Individuals who procrastinate more frequently are more likely to report lower performance and reduced work quality.

## Dataset

The dataset was collected through an online survey and includes information about:
- Procrastination frequency
- Task starting behavior
- Phone usage
- Causes of procrastination
- Self-rated work quality
- Academic/work performance

## Tools & Technologies

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

- Over 85% of respondents reported procrastinating sometimes, often, or always.
- Social media was the most common distraction.
- More than half of respondents begin tasks within 24 hours of the deadline.
- High daily phone usage was strongly associated with procrastination behavior.
- Most participants believed procrastination negatively affected their performance.
